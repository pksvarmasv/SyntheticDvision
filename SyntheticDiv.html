About This Tool

- This lesson will teach you how to divide two polynomials using synthetic division.
This lesson only deals with divisors whose leading coefficient is 1.
- To begin with, first Enter the 2 ploynomials into boxes named "Dividend" and "Divisor".
- For example, enter x^2+2x+3 for Dividend and x+4 for Divisor.
- To get the answer, click "Solve".
- For step-by-step explanation on how to solve the problem, click "Step-By-Step". You can then also use Ctrl-Alt-t as shortcut for the next step
- If you want to solve it by yourself, click "Exercise". After completing the exercise, you can check if your answer is correct by clicking "Verify". Or you can also use Ctrl-Alt-v to verify your answer.
